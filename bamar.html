Bamar

The Bamar are the majority ethnic group of Myanmar, shaping much of the nation’s culture, language, and traditions.

Culture and Traditions

Bamar culture is deeply influenced by Theravada Buddhism, which guides daily life, art, and festivals. The most iconic celebrations include Thingyan (Water Festival) and Thadingyut (Festival of Lights).

Literature, music, and dance traditions thrive, with a strong emphasis on Buddhist storytelling and classical performance arts.

Traditional Dress

Bamar men wear the longyi (sarong-like garment) with a collared shirt, while women wear longyi paired with fitted blouses.

Traditional attire is often made with colorful silk or cotton, and women commonly wear thanaka on their cheeks as a natural cosmetic and sun protection.
