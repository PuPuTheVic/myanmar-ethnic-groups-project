Kayin

The Kayin people live across southeastern Myanmar and are well known for their weaving, music, and deep cultural heritage.

Culture and Traditions

Kayin traditions are rooted in storytelling, music, and farming life. Many Kayin still practice animism, while others have adopted Buddhism and Christianity.

Their villages are famous for communal celebrations, bamboo instruments, and traditional dances performed during harvest festivals.

Traditional Dress

Kayin men traditionally wear white tunics while women wear long woven dresses often decorated with red and white patterns.

Their weaving skills are highly regarded, with each color and stripe carrying symbolic meaning related to family and identity.
