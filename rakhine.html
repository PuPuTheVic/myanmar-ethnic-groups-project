Rakhine

The Rakhine people live mainly along Myanmar’s western coast, historically known for the ancient kingdom of Mrauk U and their distinct coastal culture.

Basic Info

RAKHINE
  • Subgroups:
  • Population:
  • Languages Spoken: Rakhine language; a Tibeto-Burman language spoken in western Myanmar, primarily in the Rakhine State. ​It is closely related to and generally mutually intelligible with Burmese. ​Notably, Rakhine retains an /r/ sound that has become /j/ in Burmese. ​Rakhine utilizes the Burmese alphabet.

History & Background

  • U Ottama (1879-1939): First Political Prisoner Monk. He is one of the first figures to bravely oppose the British colonial rule and motivate the political consciousness of the Burmese people through his speeches for independence.(a notable intellectual who studied in various countries and mastered up to nine languages)
  • ​Queen Shin Saw Pu (1394-1471): the first and only queen regnant in Myanmar's history who ruled Hanthawaddy (modern-day Bago).
  • King Min Bar Gyi (1531-1553): ruled the Rakhine Kingdom at its peak, making it the most powerful and glorious

Geography & Location

  • Main region: Rakhine State (located on the western coast of Myanmar. At the north, it shares a border with Chin State and Bangladesh and southern and western parts of the state are bordered by the Bay of Bengal)
  • Major towns: ​Thandwe (capital)
  • Tourist attractions: Mrauk U Ancient City (the last capital of the Rakhine Kingdom), Ngapali Beach, Mahamuni Buddha Image (one of the most significant Buddhist heritage sites in Rakhine)

Culture & Traditions

Their culture shares many similarities with the Bamar culture but also shows a strong influence from India, likely due totheir geographic isolation by the Arakan Mountains and their proximity to India.

  • Traditional Dress: Rakhine men typically wear a longyi, a shirt, and a turban. Women wear a longyi with traditional designs and a blouse.
  • Music & Dance:
  • Festivals: The Sangrai Festival, a water festival, is celebrated to welcome the New Year. Throwing water symbolizes washing away the dirt and bad luck of the old year to start fresh.
  • Cuisines: Rakhine cuisines are known for its bold, spicy flavors, often using seafood and chili. Rakhine Mont Di is a famous rice noodle dish popular throughout Myanmar.

Religion & Beliefs

The Rakhine people are one of the four main Buddhist ethnic groups in Myanmar.

The majority of the Rakhine people are Theravada Buddhists. They also hold some traditional beliefs, such as nat worship, which often coexist with their Buddhist practices.
